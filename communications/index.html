Communications

The chapter communicates with its members via the following channels:

  1. Social Media
  2. Slack
  3. Website chat
  4. Email
  5. How To Send Emails

Responsibility: Communications Chair maintains all communications channels.

Social Media

The chapter has the following social media accounts:

  1. Twitter | @lcwics | https://twitter.com/lcwics
  2. Instagram | @lcwics | https://instagram.com/lcwics/
  3. Facebook | @lcwics | http://facebook.com/lcwics

Authentication for Twitter and Instagram accounts are stored in LastPass. Access each account by logging into LastPass with the proper credentials. Facebook requires a current admin in the FB group to add you to the page.

Managing Multiple Social Media Accounts

To facitilate multi-channel posting and make life easier, install the Buffer app on your mobile device or log in online using the lcwics Twitter credentials.

With Buffer, you can cross-post on Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram once and the same post will appear on all channels.

Posting Frequency

Posting frequency is up to the Communications Chair in agreement with the executive board. Ideally, posting to announce events and post-event recaps and photos are highly suggested to keep members and followers up-to-date.

Slack

The chapter uses Slack chat for real-time communication with members, advisors, and executive board:

  • lehmanwics.slack.com

Ask a current board member to send you the invite link to our Slack or find it in bit.ly by accessing the credentials via LastPass. A current board member should add you to the private e-board chat and make you an admin in the workspace.

Admins can manage settings, integrations, and membership for the Slack workspace.

Important items can be pinned to a channel and will stay pinned forever.

Make sure to remove admin privileges at the end of each semester as board members leave.

Important Channels

New members are automatically added to the general, opportunities, events, and _announcements channels. The channels are used as follows:

  • general : Posting general content to share with members, news, clips, reposts from other channels, etc.
  • opportunities : Posting internships, jobs, resources for interviewing, and anything else that could help lead to an opportunity
  • events : Posting upcoming events (preference for women-focused, women-led events)
  • _announcements : Posting important notifications for all members. Use @everyone to notify everyone in the workspace

Website Chat

The chapter uses an integrated chat bot on our website to communicate with anyone who is on our website and has questions.

The service used for this chat bot is called smallchat and is an app connected to a private Slack channel called: #lcwics_site_chat

The Communications chair is responsible for responding to chat conversations on the website as soon as possible.

Email

The chapter uses the following email services:

  1. Mailchimp
  2. Gmail | lehmanwics@gmail.com

To log into Mailchimp, access the credentials via LastPass. The Gmail login is to be provided to you by current or previous board members.

Mailchimp is used to send announcements, newsletters, meeting notes, and event reminders to our members. Gmail is used to communicate with members who contact us directly, respond to emails, communicate with advisors and faculty, as well as communicate with sponsors.

Sharing Mailbox

The Gmail inbox is currently shared amongst all board members. If a member needs to be contacted or replied to directly, the email should be prefixed with their title and name.

Example: @COMM/Keri - "Email subject here"

This allows for the correct board member to review an email. For all new emails that come into the inbox without direction, the Comm chair will respond and maintain contact until the end of the thread or a new thread begins.

Once an email thread is completed or has ended and no further communication is necessary, please archive the email.

Member Communication

The Google Form titled "Lehman WiCS Welcome Form" is connected to Mailchimp service via Zapier as a zap called "Welcome Form to MailChimp Connection".

When someone signs up using the Google Form, an action is fired off to Zapier and the new member is automatically added to Mailchimp List called "Lehman WiCS". Once the new member is added, a welcome email titled 'Welcome Message' under Campaigns is sent to the user.

This Welcome email should be reviewed and edited at the beginning of each semester to maintain accurate information.

Do not manually add any members to this list as it could corrupt the connection.

Faculty/Advisor Communication

Faculty and advisors should not be emailed directly via Mailchimp. All faculty, staff, and advisors should be emailed directly from our Gmail mailbox.

Sending/Receiving Emails

Gmail

As explained above, the chapter uses Gmail and Mailchimp for communication. When sending an email from the lehmanwics@gmail.com mailbox, following the format below:

Hello ,

Content

Thank you, Lehman WiCS

An auto-reply is turned on to direct senders appropriately.

Replying to Emails

Communications Chair is reponsible for replying to all general emails following the format above. For emails directed to someone on the board, that person is responsible for replying and continuing the thread.

Mailchimp

There are three templates in Mailchimp viewable under the "Templates" folder when you log in:

  • General
  • Events & Reminders
  • Newsletter

The general template should be used for all general emails that are not part of a newsletter or event blast/event reminder. Use this sparingly as we do not want to spam our members.

The events and events reminders template should be used for any event blasts or event reminders. Follow the events section for information on when to send these email.

The Newsletter template is for sending newsletters only. See the newsletter section for information on newsletter structure and format.
