Events for the chapter are stored under the "Events" folder in Google Drive. A guide for activities to consider every year for events is also available in that folder.

An event template is available in Google Drive under "Events" folder to facilitate the process of creating an event.

Events must be pre-approved and previously agreed upon by the board. *

It is suggested that the board collectively comes up with an event title, flyer, and description before submitting an event to make the process quicker. This is usually done during semester/yearly planning.

Submitting Events

Events must be submitted at least a month in advance to secure the desired location.

AFTER YOU SUBMIT AN EVENT, GO TO MARGARET IN STUDENT LIFE & CONFIRM THE ROOM RIGHT AWAY. Avoid things falling through the cracks and not having a room in the end.

Note that food is not part of event submission.

To host events at Lehman College, the event must be submitted via Campus Link.

To submit an event:

  1. Locate the Women in Computer Science Club under "My Memberships".
  2. Select "Manage Organization" on the top-right or go to https://lehman.campuslabs.com/engage/actioncenter/organization/lcwics
  3. Click the hamburger menu (three stacked lines icon) on the top-left next to 'Women in Computer Science'
  4. Select "Events" or go to https://lehman.campuslabs.com/engage/actioncenter/organization/lcwics/events
  5. Click the "Create Event" button on the top-right
  6. Select an appropriate "Event Title", "Theme", and "Description" for the event
  7. Select the appropriate date and time for the event (schedule your event a half hour before the desired time to avoid delays)
  8. IMPORTANT Enter the desired location and room for the event (i.e. Music Building - Recital Hall) and select "No, I'd prefer not to show a map"
  9. Select appropriate "Event Details"
  10. Leave "RSVP Settings" as-is
  11. Under "Additional Information", select an alternate date if previously agreed by the board (otherwise, enter "N/A") and include a set-up time of 45 minutes before the event is set to begin
  12. Estimate the maximum number of expected attendees
  13. Click Next

On the next page: Upload a cover photo (like flyer) or skip if one is not available. Use Canva to create one.

Image size guidelines: 1024px by 600px or larger
File size limit: 10MB
Supported file types JPG, JPEG, GIF, PNG, and PDF

On the next page:

  1. For "Event Additional Information Form Host Information", enter the contact information for the person who will be available during the event or overseein the event.
  2. For "DO YOU NEED EQUIPMENT (LAPTOPS, MICROPHONES, ETC.) AND/OR FURNITURE (TABLES, CHAIRS, ETC.)?", select "Yes" if you will require a projector/screen to present or a podium/stage, etc. Select "No" if you do not need equipment.
  3. For "OUT-OF-CAMPUS EVENT?", choose "No" if the event will be at Lehman

On the next page(If you selected "yes" for needing equipment):

  1. For "AUDIO VISUAL REQUESTS", select the A/V equipment needed. If you need a projector screen, add under "Other".
  2. For "TABLES & CHAIRS", choose the amount of tables and chairs for expected attendees. Add 10 extra chairs just in case.
  3. For "SPECIAL FURNITURE", indicated any special furniture needed. A podium and stage is suggested for evening events with speakers.
  4. For "OTHER", specify any additional equipment not listed that you will need.
  5. For "ROOM SET-UP", choose a preset layout or upload a drawing of your own.

On the next page: Review event submission and add a comment if necessary. Print the confirmation that the event was submitted once you click "Submit".

Catering

Event submissions do not include food requests (unless you are using your own money, in which case, skip this and order the food you want). Food requests are made at the beginning of the semester via budget requests. See the Finances section for more information on creating budgets and requesting funds.

Catering pamphlets for Lehman College are on Campus Link and the Google Drive.

If using sponsorship funds for food instead, confirm the food request a week before the event and again 3 hours before the event. See the Finances section for more information on keeping proper financial records for food requests.

Decorations & Other Expenses

Event submissions do not include funding for decorations or other expenses. See the Finances section for more information on creating budgets and requesting funds.

Confirm shipment or delivery is en route, two weeks before the event.

If using sponsorship funds for these expenses instead, see the Finances section for more information on keeping appropriate records.

Flyers

Flyers should be disbursed around campus at least 2 weeks in advance, with 3-4 weeks being optimal. Use Canva to create your flyer and save to the Google Drive.

Send the flyer to David for printing (color and/or black & white copies). It is suggested to have at least 40, 8.5 x 11 FULL COLOR copies and 20, 8.5 x 11 quarter (flyer split into 4 flyers) FULL COLOR copies.

The quarter flyers should be used for in-class and lecturer promotions.

TIP: Contact the Student Life Building front-desk to post flyers for you around campus as they know all the popular bulletin boards at Lehman.

Promotion

There are several channels available for event promotion:

  • Mailchimp
  • Slack
  • College chairs/faculty
  • Meetings
  • In-Class Promotion

Use Mailchimp to send out meeting announcements at least 2-3 weeks before the event via newsletter. Send a reminder on the day-of in the morning (schedule the email).

Announce the event on Slack as soon as possible and add the event to the LC WiCS Calendar. Post a reminder a day before and a couple of hours before the event on Slack. Once an event is added to our Calendar, a notification immediately goes to Slack.

Send an email a week before the event to CUNY college department chairs and Lehman faculty politely asking to share details with their students. DO NOT USE mailchimp for this. It's rude to mass email them since they did not subscribe to our list. Email them one-by-one from YOUR personal email and CC LehmanWics Gmail. Include the flyer, date, time, and location.

Announce and remind members during meetings that the event is coming up.

Email professors asking for permission to promote in their classes and/or share the event flyer with their classes. For bigger turnout, choose lower-level CMP courses and higher-level electives. Consider cross-promoting and/or co-sponsoring with NSBE/SHPE and/or ALPFA or other CUNY clubs for larger turnout.

A/V Equipment

You have two options: 1. Get A/V from Student Life Building 2. Get A/V from Media Services (Migdio Dominguez)

Option 1 is best for low-key student events where high-quality equipment is not needed. As of now, the SLB A/V is outdated and may not be optimal for most events. Request it anyway in the event form as a backup.

Option 2 is your go-to option. They have high-quality equipment that works with Lehman setup. This equipment MUST be well taken care of and returned without damage. Contact Migdio Dominguez on Media Services by email (use your own email and CC Lehman WiCS or it might be marked as spam) and then call/go directly to them to confirm.

Day-Of Event Structure

Arrive at least two hours before the scheduled event time to wrangle any needed equipment, confirm the location is open, set up food, seating, and decorations.

Have a sign-up sheet ready and available to sign-in attendees.

Follow the event template to have a run-down of what needs to be completed before, during, and after the event.

Events should follow the same general format:

  1. A welcoming statement, usually by the chapter Chair, to welcome attendees and to talk briefly about the chapter
  2. Introduction of the speaker(s) [if applicable]
  3. Introduce the talk
  4. Begin event
  5. A question and answer period
  6. a closing statement to thank the speaker(s) for the program and announce the next meeting

A thank you note and small gift should be sent to speakers after the event to thank them for their contribution.

Remember to take photos and do a recap!

After each event, the Communications Chair should add up any points for members and send an email to those not in our mailing list.
